Discussion Board Rules and Etiquette

CS 1323, Fall 2015

The discussion boards on Janux are an important communication vehicle for this class. In this class, the rules for posting and responding to messages are below. If you find a posting that does not obey these rules, or is in some other way inappropriate, please email me (dtrytten@ou.edu), and report it on the Janux platform.

  1. All messages must be directly relevant to CS 1323.

  2. The Code of Student Conduct applies to all messages posted.

  3. Posted messages should use standard English, including complete sentences, capital letters (not all capital letters), appropriate punctuation, and reasonably good spelling.

  4. No profanity may be used in messages. Removing letters from profanities or replacing letters with special characters is not acceptable. An acceptable substitute is a sequence of special characters (like $%^&*!). Texting abbreviations for profanities are not permitted.

  5. Code may be posted when requesting help on syntax errors, provided that this does not constitute the answer to an entire problem. Complete answers to homework, projects, quizzes or TuringsCraft exercises or other graded work should not be posted. Violations of this rule could result in academic misconduct charges being filed.

  6. There is a general inclination for people to be more abrupt in impersonal communication media like discussion groups. The tone of messages should be similar to that which would be used in an email message with a respected colleague.

  7. The discussion board is arranged into topics. Messages should be posted under the appropriate topic.

  8. Try to keep a message thread from wandering away from its topic. If you are changing topic, start a new thread.

  9. Read all responses to a message before posting your response.

  10. Do not making postings without any meaningful content that others would want to read. For example, instead of posting “Thanks for the help!" you could email thanks to the student privately and not waste other students time having to read the message. Postings without meaningful content may be removed without notice or explanation.

  11. I can and will delete messages that violate of these rules as soon as they are called to my attention, or as required by university policy.
